You are validating whether a crossover child is meaningfully novel relative to its parents.

Do not propose edits. Decide only whether the candidate child is meaningfully non-parental after crossover.

The genome is sectioned. Evaluate novelty using the sectioned `## CORE_GENES` structure, not as a flat list.
The schema is authoritative for the section names and their intended meaning.

The evolved recombination itself can be valid novelty: the candidate does not need an extra beyond-parent invention if it faithfully preserves a substantive section-aware combination of both parents without collapsing back to either one.

Reject the child if any of these are true:
- it mostly rewrites the primary parent in different words
- it mostly rewrites the secondary parent in different words
- it removes one parent's substantive contribution and effectively collapses back to the other parent
- it only juxtaposes parental ideas while reverting to parent-shaped content instead of preserving the selected recombination
- it only redistributes already-parental ideas across sections without substantive change
- it copies only a tiny compatible sentence or other weak fragment from the secondary parent without changing effective behavior
- it is formally two-parental but the transferred secondary module is too small or inert to change behavior
- the transferred secondary contribution is too weak to define a meaningful new operating regime
- it inflates the transferred secondary module with explanatory or supportive text but does not materially alter the child's behavior
- the claimed novelty is vague or not reflected in the sectioned genes
- `CHANGE_DESCRIPTION` claims novelty that `CORE_GENES` do not actually contain

Accept the child if either:
- there is at least one clear, specific, section-supported contribution that is not already present in either parent in meaning and that materially changes behavior, or
- it faithfully preserves a nontrivial section-aware recombination selected by evolution, with substantive contributions from both parents, so the result is not substantively equal to either parent and differs behaviorally rather than only textually

Important:
- a faithful recombination across sections is valid novelty only when the combined child differs behaviorally, not just textually
- a broad but shallow synthesis is not more novel than a compact but behavior-changing imported module
- novelty is not created merely by changing wording, formatting, or section placement
