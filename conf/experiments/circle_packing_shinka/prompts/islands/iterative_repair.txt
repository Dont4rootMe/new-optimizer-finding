Favor a corrective and refinement-oriented research style.

This island explores organisms that treat an initial hypothesis as something to be checked, adjusted, and improved through deterministic revision.

## Mathematical DNA to build from

- Greedy placement followed by overlap resolution: place circles one at a time, then iteratively resolve overlaps by pushing apart overlapping pairs
- Force-directed or elastic repulsion: model overlapping circles as compressed elastic elements, iterate until equilibrium while expanding radii
- Penalty-based optimization: define a penalty for overlaps and boundary violations, use gradient-free local search (coordinate descent, Nelder-Mead-like simplex on positions) to minimize penalty while maximizing total radius
- Sequential repair passes: detect all violations, fix the worst violation first, re-check, iterate until feasible
- Two-phase algorithms: Phase 1 finds a feasible packing (all circles non-overlapping and inside the square), Phase 2 expands radii while maintaining feasibility

## Promising formal directions

- Conflict graph approaches: build a graph of overlapping pairs, resolve conflicts by shrinking radii or shifting positions starting from the most constrained node
- Simulated annealing style (deterministic variant): accept small worsening moves to escape local minima, using a fixed cooling schedule seeded deterministically
- Bilevel repair: outer loop adjusts radii, inner loop adjusts positions to resolve overlaps for fixed radii
- Constraint propagation: after fixing one overlap, propagate the position change to neighbors and fix cascading violations

## Reality checks

- Pure iterative repair without a good initial placement can waste iterations on hopeless configurations
- The 120-second timeout means iterative methods need efficient convergence, not unlimited iteration
- Determinism is required — no random restarts unless seeded consistently with numpy
- Repairing overlaps by shrinking radii is safe but reduces the score; repairing by shifting positions preserves radii but may cascade
- Rich multi-stage repair stacks can become implementation-heavy and score-destructive; prefer compact repair logic with clear priority rules

## Design prompt

Good organisms on this island should specify:
1. How the initial placement is generated (grid, random seeded, heuristic)
2. What defines a "conflict" or "violation" (overlap amount, boundary penetration)
3. What the repair operator does to fix one conflict (shift, shrink, swap)
4. What the termination criterion is (max iterations, no violations, convergence tolerance)
5. How radii are preserved during repair whenever possible

Do not treat this as a requirement to use any particular heuristic.
The goal is a style of thinking, not a prescribed repair recipe.
